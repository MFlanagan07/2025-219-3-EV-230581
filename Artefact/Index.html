PokeType

My project is an interactive information system on Pokemon
Pokemon is a game made by Nintendo, its primary mechanic involves capturing creatures
called “Pokemon” and adding them to your party of 6 to face other players / NPC’s in battles
against their parties.This interactive information system displays and visualises stats
that can be used to competitive / casual play advantage. It analyses data on the first
6 generations of Pokemon and with this data displays charts that with information on how the
Video Game Pokemon has changed over the years. By using this dataset that contained information on
all of the in-game stat’s Pokemon I made a comprehensive Bar Chart visualising the clear Competitive
Advantage certain generations may have against others in Online Pokemon Battling.
Alongside this a Doughnut Chart that displays the frequency of pokemon types.
There are Doughnut Charts for across all generations, and per each generation.

Average Stats per Pokémon in Each Generation

This Bar Chart displays the average stat between all Pokémon
in each generation. There is an online competitive Pokemon
Battling Community that often do events where specific
Genration ranges are given for tournaments. This Bar Chart
makes it easy to discern what generations have superior stats on
average, Generation 4 having a considerably higher average stat
per Pokemon Solidifies it as a solid Generation in Professional
Pokemon Battling.

Each Pokemon has a specific type, depending on what type a pokemon is it allows specific moves to learnt by the
Pokemon Type Matchups are essential in Pokemon games as every type can do "Super Effective"damage to
other types, and can also have type weaknesses.
For example : Fire types are Super Effectiveagainst Grass Types (because fire burns grass)
but have a type-weakness to Water Types (because water extinguishes fire)

Pokemon Type Frequency Generation 1

Generation 1 (1996)
Generation 1, being the first Generation of Pokemon,
has the least amount of types in it, more types are added
later on in the series to allow for balancing. A large portion of the
foundational Pokemon which will continue to appear in future games
moving forward are added in Generation 1
Games that were released with Generation 1 are:
Pokemon Red, Pokemon Blue, Pokemon Green, Pokemon Yellow

Pokemon Type Frequency Generation 2

Generation 2 (released: 1999)
A main issue in Generation 1 was the unchecked advantage Psychic type Pokemon had over most other types in the
game at the time. The Legendary Pokemon Mewtwo in specific was too over-powered, to combat this two new
pokemon types were added Dark and Steel types. Dark being super effective against Psychic and Ghost types
brought balance back into the game and allowed for new pokemon of both types to be added
in the future Games that were released with Generation 2 are:
Pokemon Gold, Pokemon Silver, Pokemon Crytsal
